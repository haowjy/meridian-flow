Chapter 47: The Campfire

The fire had burned low by the time Sera stopped talking. Embers drifted upward like small prayers, and Kael watched them vanish against the darkness of the canopy above.

"Wake me for second watch," she said, pulling the cloak tighter around her shoulders. Her voice was already half-gone, softened by exhaustion and the particular quiet that descends after hard truths have been spoken aloud.

He nodded, though she couldn't see it. The fire popped. Somewhere in the valley below, a river moved through stone.

He counted her breaths the way a man counts coins he owes. Each one steady, trusting, unearned. The firelight caught the edge of her jaw, and he looked away before the guilt could settle into something he'd have to name.

When the second watch came, he didn't wake her. He sat with the silence and the dark and the weight of what he was about to do, and he let her have one more hour of not knowing.
