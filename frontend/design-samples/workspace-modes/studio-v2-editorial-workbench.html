Ch. 47 The Campfire
In ReviewAgent: Continuity

The fire had burned low by the time Sera stopped talking. Embers drifted upward like small prayers, and Kael watched them vanish against the darkness of the ridgeline.

"Wake me for second watch," she said, pulling the cloak tighter around her shoulders. Her voice was already half-gone, softened by the kind of exhaustion that comes after honesty.

He nodded, though she couldn't see it. For a long while he sat motionless, listening to the rhythm of her breathing as it slowed and steadied. He counted her breaths the way a man counts coins he owes.

* * *

Dawn came without announcement. The fire was ash. Sera woke to find Kael already standing at the overlook, his back to camp, studying the valley below as if measuring something only he could see.

"You didn't wake me," she said.

"You needed the rest." He didn't turn. Somewhere below them, the river threaded between stones, patient and indifferent as geography.
